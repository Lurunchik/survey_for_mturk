This HIT is an academic experiment on humor evaluation.

Thank you for your interest in the experiment of humor evaluation. Chatbots and mobile personal assistants are becoming popular and ubiquitous mobile applications. Often, developers of such applications want to make them to behave more ‘human-like’, and inject humorous responses into human—computer conversation. However, sense of humor and appropriateness of a humorous response are highly subjective and context-dependent. The current study aims at a large-scale evaluation of humorous dialogs.

Following this, you will be shown 30 dialog jokes, i.e. jokes consisting of a question (Q) and an answer (A). You are asked to rate each joke on a 4-point scale:  not funny at all (1),  can be better (2),  funny (3),  hilarious (4). Please note that the jokes were collected from the Web with minimum filtering, so they may be rude or contain obscene language. Towards the end of the experiment, you will also be asked to answer some questions about yourself. The whole duration of the experiment should be around 10 minutes.

This experiment was approved by URFU's Social Sciences Research Board. Your participation in the experiment is completely voluntary and you are free to withdraw from the experiment at any time. Your data will be treated confidentially and you are also free to request your data to be removed. We do not get access to your personal details. If you have any questions regarding the study, please feel free to contact Dr. Pavel Braslavski from the URFU (Pavel.Braslavsky@urfu.ru).

By clicking the 'Next' button below you accept the conditions of the experiment and agree to participate. After completing this HIT, you will receive your reward plus a bonus payment that is based on how you play the experiment.
